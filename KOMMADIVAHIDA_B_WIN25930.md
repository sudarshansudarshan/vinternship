I Did Not Become Faster — I Became Stronger 

At the beginning of this Vinternship journey, I had a quiet belief that learning meant speed.

Finishing lessons quickly. Understanding everything in one go. Moving ahead without stopping.

But very slowly, and very honestly, this internship changed that belief.

There were days when I sat in front of my screen and felt completely stuck. Not because the problem was impossible — but because my mind was still learning how to think.

In those moments, I realised something important.

This journey was not teaching me how to write better code. It was teaching me how to stay with confusion.

Through ViBe sessions, case studies, and discussions, I noticed how often I wanted quick answers. Yet the real learning happened only when I allowed myself to struggle — without rushing to escape it.

One of the most powerful changes I felt was in how I look at mistakes.

Earlier, mistakes felt personal. Now, they feel informational.

They tell me what I still need to learn.

Preparing for viva, working through case studies, and observing how developers approach problems showed me something very different from classroom learning.

Here, no one was waiting for perfection. They were waiting for effort, clarity of thought, and honesty in trying.

There was also a silent lesson hidden inside this journey — consistency.

Not the dramatic kind. Not long hours. Not motivational quotes.

Just showing up again and again.

Some days I understood more. Some days I barely understood anything.

But I kept showing up.

And slowly, my fear of being “not good enough” started becoming smaller than my willingness to learn.

This Vinternship did not make me faster.

It made me steadier.

It made me more patient with myself.

It taught me that growth does not always look confident. Sometimes, it looks like quietly staying — what if you feel unsure.

And maybe that is the strongest skill I will carry forward from this journey.

Sudarshan Iyengar
