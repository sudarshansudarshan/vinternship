At the beginning of my Vinternship, I was afraid of asking questions.
Not because I had none.
But because I was scared that my questions would sound too simple.

I waited.
I re-read instructions.
I searched quietly.
I tried to fix things on my own.
Sometimes this helped.

But many times, it only made my confusion heavier.

This journey slowly taught me something unexpected.

Good learners are not the ones who already know more.

They are the ones who learn how to ask better questions.

During sessions, case studies and group discussions, I noticed how powerful one honest question could be.

Not a smart question.
Not a polished question.
Just an honest one.

‚ÄúWhy does this behave this way?‚Äù
‚ÄúWhere am I going wrong in my thinking?‚Äù
When I finally started asking, something changed.

The answers helped, yes.
But more importantly, my thinking became clearer.
I stopped hiding what I did not understand.
I started naming it.
I realised that confusion is not weakness.
It is information.
Preparing for viva made this even more real.

It was not enough to memorise steps or solutions.
I had to explain ideas.
And whenever I could not explain something, I knew exactly what I needed to learn next.
This Vinternship did not only give me technical exposure.
It gave me the courage to be a beginner.
To speak uncertainty without embarrassment.
To learn without pretending.

Today, I value questions more than quick fixes.
Because I learned that every strong solution begins with one clear question.
