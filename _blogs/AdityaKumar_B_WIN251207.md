When I began my virtual internship in the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express.js, React, Node.js), I carried a sense of confidence from my prior coding experiences. In fact, I’ll admit — I was a little overconfident. I thought I already knew enough to handle anything that came my way.

But this vinternship turned out to be a true eye‑opener.

Lessons Learned
- Depth Over Surface: I realized that knowing syntax or building small projects is very different from designing scalable, production‑ready applications.
- Humility in Learning: Overconfidence can blind us. This experience reminded me that every technology has layers of complexity waiting to be explored.
- Real‑World Challenges: Debugging APIs, handling database transactions, and managing state in React taught me patience and problem‑solving beyond textbooks.
- Collaboration & Feedback: Working virtually with mentors and peers showed me the importance of clear communication and openness to constructive criticism.

Why This Matters
This vinternship didn’t just sharpen my MERN stack skills — it reshaped my mindset. I now approach coding with curiosity instead of assumption, and with humility instead of overconfidence.

Looking Ahead
I’m excited to keep building projects, refining my backend and frontend skills, and eventually blending this foundation with AI/ML to design scalable intelligent systems.
