When I first stepped into the NPTEL Vinternship, excitement wasn’t the dominant emotion — uncertainty was.

A completely new platform. An unfamiliar way of learning. A different rhythm altogether.

In the beginning, it felt mentally heavy. I was used to clearly structured courses where the path was straightforward. Here, I had to figure things out, adapt, and recalibrate how I approached learning. The initial days were slightly overwhelming, but instead of resisting the process, I chose to experience it as it unfolded.

That small shift in mindset made a big difference.

The ViBe platform changed how I engaged with concepts. Rather than racing through modules just to complete them, I began spending time understanding what I was actually building. The hands-on approach demanded attention and reflection. It wasn’t about finishing tasks — it was about thinking through them.

Slowly, confusion gave way to curiosity.

The case studies were a turning point. At first glance, they seemed intense. But as I worked through them, I realized they weren’t designed to test what I memorized — they were shaping how I approached problems. They pushed me to apply ideas, experiment with different strategies, and accept mistakes as part of the learning curve. Often, the most valuable lessons came from moments when things didn’t work the first time.

The standup sessions added another dynamic layer to the journey. They brought structure, accountability, and a sense of momentum. The Kahoot quizzes were particularly engaging — fast-paced, competitive, and revealing. They highlighted not just what I knew, but how well I could recall and apply it under pressure. Learning felt interactive rather than isolated.

Being part of the Dijkstrians Cohort made the experience even more meaningful. Everyone was navigating their own challenges, yet we were progressing together. Seeing peers grow, struggle, and persist created a shared motivation that’s hard to replicate in solo learning environments.

Over time, I began noticing subtle changes in myself. I became more deliberate in my thinking. I paused before diving into solutions. I grew more comfortable sitting with complex problems instead of rushing to resolve them. Learning stopped feeling transactional and started feeling exploratory.

This Vinternship didn’t transform me overnight. Instead, it sparked gradual shifts — in discipline, in perspective, and in confidence. The growth was steady and quiet, noticeable only when I reflected on how far I had come.

What started as uncertainty slowly evolved into genuine interest.

I would like to sincerely thank Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar , Prof. Nandan Prabhudesai , and the entire Vicharanashala (Lab for Education Design) team for designing such a thoughtful learning environment. From ViBe learning and case studies to standups, quizzes, and continuous guidance, every element contributed to this experience.

I’m equally grateful to my fellow interns whose collaboration and energy made the experience more enriching.

Sometimes progress doesn’t begin with clarity. Sometimes it begins the moment you decide to embrace the process.
