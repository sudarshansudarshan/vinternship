I used to think learning meant understanding things quickly. 

If I could follow along, complete the task, and see the result on my screen, I believed I was learning. For a long time, that definition worked for me.

The Vinternship changed that definition completely.

When the journey began with hands-on MERN stack learning through ViBe, I felt confident. Watching, coding along, and building small pieces felt familiar. But the real shift happened when we moved beyond just following and started thinking.

The case studies were different. 

There were moments when I stared at the problem and had no idea where to begin. Not because I hadn’t seen the concepts before, but because this time there was no step-by-step guidance. No one was telling me which function to write or which pattern to use. There were days when I questioned whether I actually understood the fundamentals. Days when debugging took more time than writing the code. Days when discussions in breakout rooms showed me how differently others were thinking.

But slowly, something changed. Instead of searching immediately for answers, I started sitting with the problem longer. Instead of measuring progress by how quickly I finished, I began measuring it by how deeply I understood. Instead of fearing feedback, I started seeing it as a chance to improve.

Not but not the least that I would like to thank Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar , Vicharanashala (Lab for Education Design) and ANNAM.AI for giving me this wonderful opportunity to be a part of this vinternship.
