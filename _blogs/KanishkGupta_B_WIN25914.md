I entered this internship believing learning was mostly about exposure—watching, absorbing, moving on. That belief didn’t last long.

Learning here demanded presence. Not through pressure, but in a way that made disengagement obvious—even to myself. There were moments when I realized I hadn’t truly understood things I thought I had. The discomfort was new, but it quickly became one of the most valuable parts of the experience.

Focused, intentional learning changed how I approached difficulty. Instead of rushing to finish, I learned to slow down and sit with confusion. Questions stopped feeling like tests and started feeling like invitations—to think deeper, reflect, and try again.

This internship quietly shifted my priorities. Consistency mattered more than intensity. Attention mattered more than speed. Learning stopped being passive and became an active responsibility.

Even after sessions ended, that shift stayed with me. I no longer measure learning by completion, but by clarity.

If I had to describe this experience in one line: it doesn’t try to impress you—it asks something of you. And that’s what truly changes how you learn.

Thanks Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design, VLED Labs Sudarshan Iyengar for the learning design and mentorship.
