# My First Web Development Experience Through MERN Stack

Commencing the Vinternship on January 22, 2026, I entered with preconceptions shaped by my AIML coursework—focused on machine learning models and data analysis. Transitioning to the MERN stack introduced unanticipated challenges that fundamentally altered my approach to professional development.

## The Learning starts...
Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar's opening standup emphasised proactive initiative over passive opportunity. The ViBe course, modelled on Vikramaditya’s riddles, demanded immediate application through concise video lectures and quizzes. Mentored by Nandan Prabhudesai, concepts from JavaScript fundamentals to React state management became tangible, though persistent debugging sessions tested resolve. Achieving rank 13 by February 13 underscored the value of sustained effort over innate aptitude.

## Progressing through the Case studies
Subsequent case studies required independent problem-solving, such as integrating MongoDB with Express for dynamic dashboards. Breakout room collaborations and guidance in live sessions from Riya Mehta honed analytical skills, transforming initial setbacks into functional prototypes. Real project contributions, including graph-based features, reinforced iterative refinement akin to ML optimisation, with viva preparations enhancing conceptual depth.

## Overall experience
This experience transcended technical acquisition, fostering a disciplined mindset. While AIML emphasises theoretical modelling, MERN cultivated deployment, practicality and collaborative execution—essential for industry relevance. Difficulties in reconciling domains yielded clarity: growth emerges from deliberate persistence amid ambiguity.

## Vote of thanks.
I am grateful to Vicharanashala (Lab for Education Design), ANNAM.AI, Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar and NPTEL for this structured journey—sincere appreciation to Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar, Nandan Prabhudesai, Riya Mehta, Sakshi Sharma and fellow interns for invaluable support.
