I received this internship opportunity through the NPTEL course The Joy of Computing Using Python. Honestly, I was not expecting this opportunity at all. When I first received the form to select my availability dates, I chose January 22nd batch.

Later, I came to know that the internship for my batch was unpaid. Many of my friends questioned my decision. They asked, “Why would you do a 10-week internship without being paid?” But for me, It was about the experience, the exposure, and the knowledge I would gain. I chose to see it as an investment in myself.

During the orientation session, Sudarshan Sir spoke about this mindset and said, “Don’t be penny smart and pound foolish.” That line truly stayed with me. If I had stepped back before, I would have missed this wonderful journey.

At the beginning, I was completely confused. I had no idea how to start. I was new to the MERN stack and had very little knowledge about it. Everything felt unfamiliar - especially the ViBe platform we were introduced to. It was entirely new to me. It was challenging. A little frustrating at first. But at the same time, it felt exciting and meaningful.

Talking about the ViBe platform - it completely changed the way I learn. We were given course content videos, but there was no option to skip, forward, or rewind. Every 2–3 minutes, there were quizzes based on the video content. If we answered incorrectly, we had to rewatch the video until we got it right.

This meant zoning out was not an option. Full concentration was required. And honestly, that helped me a lot. I am someone who easily zones out during lectures, but this system forced me to stay attentive. The video lectures by Nandan sir were clear, structured, and easy to understand , even from the basics.

We had stand-up sessions three times a week. These sessions were one of my favourite parts. It was wonderful interacting with peers and sharing experiences.

I have always been a shy and nervous person, especially afraid of turning my camera on during online meetings. But here, they ensured everyone attended with cameras on and encouraged us to open up and share. Slowly, I started speaking. I shared my experiences. And that small step felt like a big achievement for me.

We were also given opportunity to interact with seniors. They shared their learning journeys, guided us, and supported us throughout. Their encouragement made the process smoother and more motivating.

We were introduced to something called Health Points. At first, it sounded scary because I was afraid of losing them. But soon, I realized how effective the system was. We earned health points by completing tasks on time and maintaining consistency. And also additional health points are given for helping our peers and participating actively.

There were deadlines for every activity, which helped us stay disciplined and focused. Instead of procrastinating, I started managing my time better.

Breakout rooms were used for peer interaction, and they turned out to be an amazing experience. I made really good friends with whom I could discuss doubts and share ideas. Having a supportive peer group made learning much easier and more enjoyable.

After completing the ViBe video content, we were given case studies to solve and this was honestly my favorite part of the internship. We were first introduced to a real-life problem statement ,clearly explained the scenario, the expected implementation approach, and how the concepts we learned could be applied. Then, the task was handed over to us.

This was where the real learning happened. Instead of just watching and understanding, we had to think, analyze, and implement the solution ourselves. It pushed us to apply the knowledge we had gained through the videos and quizzes. Solving these case studies made everything feel practical and meaningful.

Each time I successfully completed one, it gave me a sense of accomplishment. I truly enjoyed working on these case studies because they challenged my thinking and improved my problem-solving skills. They helped me move from simply learning concepts to actually building solutions.

We were also introduced to Discord for student interaction, doubt clarification, and announcements. It became a very helpful communication platform throughout the internship.

 The live sessions were engaging and useful. The mentors taught us everything from the very basics, which made it easier for beginners like me to follow along confidently. What I appreciated the most was their patience. They ensured that everyone understood clearly before moving forward. 

The entire team managing this internship deserves great appreciation for their enthusiasm and dedication.

Suruchi Ma’am, who conducted our stand-up sessions, was extremely encouraging. Rohit Sir, Nithin Sir, Meenakshi Ma'am ,Sakshi Ma’am, Bhavna Ma’am, and the entire team worked tirelessly to ensure everything ran smoothly without any disturbances. Their effort and passion were clearly visible.

I never imagined that my first internship - even though it was online - would give me so much knowledge, confidence, and personal growth. It was not just about learning technical skills; it was about discipline, communication, consistency, and stepping out of my comfort zone.

I am truly grateful for this opportunity and thankful to the entire team for making this journey so meaningful. VLED Labs 

This internship taught me : sometimes the best decisions are not the ones that pay you immediately, but the ones that help you grow.
