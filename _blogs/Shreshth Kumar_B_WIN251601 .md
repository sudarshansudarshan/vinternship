There is a big difference between doing an internship and growing through one. I didn’t understand that difference when I started my journey with VLED Lab. To me, it was another opportunity to learn something technical, add a skill, and move forward.



But somewhere along the way, something shifted.



At the beginning, everything felt overwhelming new tools, new frameworks and new expectations. There were moments when I questioned whether I was capable enough for this internship or not. Watching others move faster made me doubt my own pace. Sometimes I would stare at my screen, unsure whether I was stuck because the problem was difficult or because I wasn’t good enough.



And yet, every time I felt stuck, I noticed something important: I wasn’t giving up.



That realization changed how I saw myself.



This internship was not designed like a checklist. It didn’t hand-hold. It didn’t simplify everything into easy wins. Instead, it allowed us to struggle. At first, I saw struggle as a sign of weakness. Slowly, I began to see it as proof that I was stretching beyond what I already knew.



One of the biggest lessons I learned was consistency not motivation, not talent just pure consistency.



There were days when I didn’t feel productive. Days when code didn’t work. Days when feedback felt heavy. But anyway opening the laptop, reading the error again, trying one more approach that built something stronger than knowledge. It built discipline.



Another unexpected part of the journey was collaboration. Discussing ideas in breakout rooms, explaining logic to peers, listening to different approaches—these moments taught me that learning is not a competition. It’s a shared process. When someone else solved a problem differently, it didn’t reduce my understanding. It expanded it.



Looking back, I don’t just see completed modules or solved problems. I see growth in confidence. I see improved patience. I see a stronger belief in my ability to figure things out.



This internship taught me that learning is not about speed. It is about depth. It is not about perfection. It is about persistence.



And I thank Prof.Sudarshan Iyengar, 

NPTEL, Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar, VLED Labs, Vicharanashala (Lab for Education Design) for the opportunity.
