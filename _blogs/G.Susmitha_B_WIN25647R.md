When I began my Vinternship with VLED Lab, I believed learning was about understanding concepts quickly and moving forward confidently. I thought progress meant clarity—knowing the answer, writing the correct code, and completing tasks on time.

This internship quietly changed that belief.Learning the MERN stack through ViBe was fully hands-on, and that itself was a shift for me. There were no shortcuts. Some days, concepts felt clear; other days, they felt confusing no matter how many times I revisited them. Earlier, I would have seen that confusion as a weakness.Here, I learned to stay with it.

One of the strongest lessons I took away from this Vinternship was the value of consistency. Motivation wasn’t always present. Confidence fluctuated. But showing up every day—opening ViBe, attempting problems, revisiting concepts—made progress possible.Breakout room discussions helped me realize that everyone was navigating their own challenges. Hearing others express similar doubts reminded me that learning is not about being fast; it’s about being present and persistent.

after sessions ended and tasks were submitted, what stayed with me wasn’t just technical knowledge. It was a mindset.I learned not to panic when I don’t understand something immediately. I learned to trust the process. I learned that struggling is not a sign of failure, but a sign of growth.

This Vinternship didn’t just teach me the MERN stack—it reshaped how I approach learning itself. And that lesson will stay with me far beyond this journey.

Thank you: Sudarshan Iyengar sir, Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design, ANNAM.AI,IIT Ropar for this opportunity.
