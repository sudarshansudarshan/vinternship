My internship journey began on 22nd January with an orientation session. I still remember my first week clearly — I was completely confused about the internship structure, the Vibe course, and the overall environment. Everything felt new, and I wasn’t sure how things worked.

However, day by day, I started gaining clarity about the internship and its expectations.

**The First Challenge – Vibe Course**

One of my first tasks was to complete 25% of the Vibe course before the deadline. To be honest, just three days before the deadline, I had not even completed 5% because I wasn’t aware that we needed to finish 25% within that timeframe.

Realizing this, I pushed myself and successfully completed 25% of the course within just three days.

After the first week, I changed my approach. Instead of focusing only on completing tasks, I began concentrating on truly understanding the concepts being taught. I started listening carefully to every explanation, and gradually, I began to genuinely enjoy the learning process.

From that point onward, everything started moving smoothly. The internship strengthened my fundamentals and boosted my confidence.

**Activities and Engagement**

I also had activities such as updating our Zoho profile picture and posting our week 1 experience on LinkedIn

I completed these tasks as well. These activities helped me build my professional presence and grow in confidence.

Currently, I am working on my case studies. They are thoughtfully designed to encourage us to think like real developers rather than just learners. Through these case studies, I am gaining practical knowledge and improving my problem-solving skills.

**Daily Standups and Live Sessions**

we have daily standup sessions where we share our internship experiences in front of everyone. I have never missed a session because I always learn something new from them. These sessions keep me consistent, accountable, and motivated.

The daily quizzes are also very helpful as they test our understanding and keep us engaged. We also participate in breakout rooms where we interact and discuss ideas with peers, improving our collaboration and communication skills.

Every day, we have live sessions with Riya Ma’am. She not only teaches us coding from the basics but also guides us on how to write clean, efficient, and high-quality code.

While we might normally write simple code just to make things work, she teaches us how to write production-ready code. She encourages us to think beyond the basics and understand modern development practices.

She explains every concept clearly and ensures we understand the logic behind what we build. Even on days when I couldn’t attend live sessions, I made sure to catch up because I didn’t want to miss the valuable knowledge she shares.

I always complete the tasks she assigns because I strongly believe:

We will never be perfect until we practice and do things ourselves.

These practical tasks have significantly improved my coding skills and hands-on experience.

Finally, I would like to express my heartfelt gratitude to the Pinternship mentor team: Nandan Prabhudesai Sir, Sakshi Sharma Mam, Nitin Sankar Sir, Meenakshi V Mam, and Rohit Sharma Sir, for giving me this wonderful opportunity. This internship has helped me grow not only technically but also personally.

A special thanks to Prof. Sudarshan Iyengar and the team VLED Labs for your valuable guidance and for creating such a beautiful and effective learning design.

I look forward to continuing this journey of learning and growth!
