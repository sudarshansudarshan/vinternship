Some learning happens quietly. You don’t notice the change immediately, but over time you realize you’re thinking differently than before. That’s how the Vinternship at IIT Ropar’s VLED Lab felt to me.

When I started working through the ViBe platform, I expected a typical course structure where you follow instructions and move on. Instead, I was given full responsibility over my pace. Nobody was pushing me forward or checking every step. At first, that freedom felt uncomfortable. Without pressure, it becomes very easy to delay things. I had to face my own discipline and motivation more than I expected.

While learning the MERN stack, the challenge wasn’t just technical. Of course there were moments when TypeScript errors or backend logic slowed me down, but the bigger struggle was consistency. Some days everything connected easily. Other days even simple ideas felt heavier than they should. Earlier I would have taken that as proof that I wasn’t good enough. During this internship I learned to see confusion differently. It wasn’t failure. It was a sign that I needed to slow down and understand more deeply.

The case studies changed how I approach problems. There wasn’t a single correct answer waiting at the end. I had to make decisions, think about tradeoffs, and sit with uncertainty. That process was frustrating at times, but it was also empowering. I stopped thinking of tasks as assignments and started seeing them as real engineering decisions.

Interacting with others in the program helped more than I expected. Hearing people share similar struggles reminded me that learning is rarely smooth. Everyone is figuring things out in their own imperfect way. That realization removed a lot of pressure and made the process feel more human.

What will stay with me after the internship isn’t just knowledge of React, Express, MongoDB, or TypeScript. It’s patience with myself. It’s the ability to stay with a problem instead of escaping it. It’s a stronger respect for consistency over speed.

If I had to explain what made this experience different, I’d say it trusted me to take my learning seriously even when nobody was watching. That trust changed how I show up, not just in coding, but in how I approach learning in general.

I can assure i'll leave this program with technical skills, but more importantly with a mindset that I know will stay with me for a long time.

Sincere thanks to VLED Labs Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design ANNAM.AI NPTEL Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar for this wonderful oppurtunity
