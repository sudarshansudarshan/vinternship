When I first joined this internship, I thought I would immediately start working on MERN technologies. But soon I realized this experience was different. First, I had to learn MERN through the ViBe platform, and then complete case studies.

Learning MERN on ViBe was a refreshing experience. The platform didn’t overwhelm with long videos—instead, each video was just 1–2 minutes long, followed by 2–3 quiz questions. If you answered incorrectly, you had to rewatch the video before moving on. At first, this felt strict and a little irritating, but over time, it helped me focus and learn at my own pace, while still hitting deadlines.

One of the biggest lessons I took away from this internship was consistency and discipline. I made it a point to attend every meeting, complete some portion of course videos every day, and stick to the schedule I had set a day before. This daily routine, though simple, brought immense satisfaction. Completing 100% of the ViBe course gave me immense joy, a sense of achievement, and confidence that I could carry the same clarity and focus into case studies and future work.

Quizzes during Zoom sessions helped me recollect and strengthen what I had learned on ViBe. Our educator made learning engaging by connecting concepts to real-world problems and showing how MERN technologies could solve them. I remember repeatedly getting some quiz questions wrong. Initially, it was frustrating, but after multiple attempts and watching the video explanations, my doubts cleared and I gained a strong understanding of the topics.

Talking to fellow interns in Zoom breakout rooms was relaxing and fun. It gave a sense of community, making the learning process less lonely and more enjoyable.

Moving on to the case studies, each question was carefully structured. Background information and examples were provided to make coding smoother. The questions started easy and gradually increased in difficulty, which helped me build confidence in applying MERN skills. The mentor constantly motivated us, conducted sessions, and encouraged discussions, which made learning even more effective.

This internship wasn’t just about coding; it was about learning, thinking, failing, and growing. Most importantly, it taught me that discipline and consistent effort, no matter how small each day, can lead to big results. The path to mastery isn’t always straight, but when structured thoughtfully, it’s deeply rewarding.

I want to thank to VLED Labs , Rohit Sharma , Sakshi Sharma , Nandan Prabhudesai , Riya Mehta , Harshdeep R. , Nitin Sankar for their guidance, encouragement, and patience throughout this journey.
